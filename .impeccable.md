## Design Context

### Users
Dual audience: technical recruiters evaluating internship/junior candidates, and startup founders/SMBs seeking a freelance web developer. Both groups view the portfolio on desktop during business hours or evening browsing. Recruiter needs to understand scope and quality of work fast. Client needs to feel confidence and see real delivered projects.

### Brand Personality
**3 words: sharp, dual-natured, deliberate.**
Andrés builds systems AND tests their limits (software + cybersecurity). The brand is not a hacker archetype — it's a precise, curious craftsman who understands both construction and vulnerability.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Sistemas y Sigilo" — precision meets curiosity.**
- Dark theme: navy-tinted blacks (not neutral gray-blacks). Security work happens at night, dashboards live in dark rooms.
- Accent: **warm amber** — completely unexpected for a dev portfolio, signals craft and deliberate thinking over hacker-neon clichés.
- Typography: **Barlow Condensed** (Black/ExtraBold) for headings — industrial precision, massive presence. **Karla** for body — slightly quirky grotesque with genuine character. JetBrains Mono used sparingly only in genuine code contexts.
- Layout: left-aligned hero, asymmetric compositions, generous whitespace.
- NO terminal tropes as decoration ($ prompts, blinking cursors used aggressively, monospace everywhere).
- NO neon green glows.

### Design Principles
1. **Craftsmanship over cleverness** — every detail deliberate, nothing decorative unless it earns its place
2. **Dual identity expressed structurally** — build and break, backend and security, always both
3. **Amber as differentiator** — one unexpected color choice does more than any animation
4. **Typography leads** — Barlow Condensed at large sizes IS the design. Guard that space.
5. **Content over chrome** — the projects are the portfolio. Design serves them, not itself
